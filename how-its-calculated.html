Siren Probability

Dashboard Map How it's calculated

This estimate is not an official forecast. It uses Pikud HaOref history and combines four factors per region: (1) time since the last siren in the region, (2) how that compares to the region’s average gap between sirens, (3) which day of the week it is (historically busier or quieter), and (4) which hour of the day it is (least likely hours give less, most likely give more). Data is at city level; we treat events in the same region within a 10-minute window as one siren incident so a single alarm hitting multiple cities (e.g. Gush Dan) counts once. Gaps, day-of-week, and time-of-day use the last 7 days; “last siren” uses the full history window.

Time since last alarm (region)

Minutes since the most recent siren in that region.

Contribution ramps linearly from 0 (before 30 minutes) to a maximum by 8 hours; that raw value is scaled to a share (max 15 points) and Gaussian-smoothed so the number doesn’t jump abruptly when a new siren occurs. The ramp start and full contribution times are configurable (timeSinceRampStartMin, timeSinceFullMin).

Average time between alarms (region)

Average gap (minutes) between consecutive siren incidents in the region over the last 7 days. Incidents are collapsed with a 20-minute debounce per region (debounceWindowMs), so one alarm that hits several cities counts as one.

We use the ratio ratio = minutesSinceLast / averageGap. Contribution grows with this ratio and is capped when you’re at 2× the average (full contribution): share = 45 × min(ratio / 2, 1) (45 is the share cap, gapShareMax).

When you’re under the average (less time has passed since the last siren than usual), the contribution is lower; as you approach the average, it rises. When you’re over the average (more time has passed than usual), the contribution is higher—the model treats that as increased risk.

Day of week (region pattern)

From the last 7 days, we count sirens per weekday (Sunday–Saturday) in the region. The current weekday gets a share from 0 to 10: the least likely weekday gets 0, the most likely gets the full 10. If all weekdays have the same count, the factor is 0.5. Share cap is dayOfWeekShareMax (10).

Time of day (region pattern)

All 24 hours are used. From the last 7 days we count sirens per hour (0–23) in the region. Every hour gets a share between a minimum and maximum: the least likely (quietest) hour gets about 2%, the most likely (busiest) gets the full 30%, and hours in between scale so that more likely hours get a higher share. Config: dayShareMin (2), dayShareMax (30).

How the total is calculated

Total probability is the sum of the four shares, then clamped: probability = clamp(timeSinceShare + gapShare + dayOfWeekShare + dayShare) to the range [0.5, 99.4] (minP, maxP). Only the time-since share is Gaussian-smoothed; the others are not.

The breakdown in the dashboard shows these four shares; their sum is the value before clamping. Max possible from each: time since 15, average gap 45, day of week 10, time of day 30 (total 100).

Data and region

Events are assigned to regions by distance to the region center. Within each region, events within 20 minutes are merged into one incident so regions with more cities don’t get inflated counts. Stats (gaps, weekday counts, hourly counts) use only incidents in that region; when a region has no events we fall back to nationwide data. Data source: last 7 days for gaps, day-of-week, and time-of-day; full history window for “last siren” and the event list.
