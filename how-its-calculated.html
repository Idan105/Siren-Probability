Siren Probability

Dashboard Map How it's calculated

This estimate is not an official forecast. It uses Pikud HaOref history and combines three factors per region: (1) time since the last siren in the region, (2) how that compares to the region’s average gap between sirens, and (3) whether the current hour is historically a peak time for alarms. Gaps and time-of-day patterns use data from the last 7 days; “last siren” uses the full history window.

Time since last alarm (region)

Minutes since the most recent siren in that region.

Contribution ramps linearly from 0 (before 30 minutes) to a maximum by 8 hours; that raw value is scaled to a share (e.g. max 20 points) and Gaussian-smoothed so the number doesn’t jump abruptly when a new siren occurs. The ramp start and full contribution times are configurable (timeSinceRampStartMin, timeSinceFullMin).

Average time between alarms (region)

Average gap (minutes) between consecutive sirens in the region over the last 7 days (same place key: city + rounded lat/lon).

We use the ratio ratio = minutesSinceLast / averageGap. Contribution grows with this ratio and is capped when you’re at 2× the average (full contribution): contribution = 65 × min(ratio / 2, 1) (65 is the share cap and is configurable as gapShareMax).

When you’re under the average (less time has passed since the last siren than usual), the contribution is lower; as you approach the average, it rises. When you’re over the average (more time has passed than usual), the contribution is higher—the model treats that as increased risk.

Time of day (region pattern)

From the last 7 days, we take the top 5 hours (0–23) with the most sirens in that region. If the current hour is one of those, it gets a multiplier on the base risk (rank 1 = strongest, rank 5 = weakest); off-peak the factor is 1.0.

Weight comes from peakHourWeights by rank; the multiplier is 1 + (weight × timeOfDayFactorRange). The dashboard breakdown shows the time-of-day effect as an additive equivalent: (factor − 1) × baseRisk.

How the total is calculated

Base risk is the sum of the first two shares (time-since and gap): baseRisk = timeSinceShare + gapShare. Only the time-since share is smoothed; the gap share is not.

Total probability is the base risk multiplied by the time-of-day factor, then clamped: probability = clamp(baseRisk × timeOfDayFactor) to the range [0.5, 99.4] (minP, maxP).

The breakdown percentages you see in the dashboard are the three shares (time-since, gap, and the additive equivalent of time-of-day); their sum equals the final number before clamping.

Data and region

Events are assigned to regions by distance to the region center. Stats (gaps, peak hours) use only events in that region; when a region has no events we fall back to nationwide data. Data source: last 7 days for gaps and time-of-day; full history window for “last siren” and the event list.
